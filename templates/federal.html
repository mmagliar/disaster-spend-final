Federal Level Spending:

A look at the numbers

From Jan. 1st, 2000 to June 6th, 2019, how much money has been spent on disaster relief and what is that money used for?

 Using all of the data obtained from OpenFEMA the following charts visualize how the United States allocates funds for disaster relief.

 The first chart contains two line graphs depicting the cumulative number of disasters and the amount spent on disasters country wide. By looking at these graphs in conjunction we can infer what years had particularly costly disasters. For example, 2005 and 2012 have major peaks in amount spent while the number of disasters did not spike, indicating large amounts of money spent per disaster. This makes sense as three major hurricanes (Ivan, Rita, and Katrina) made landfall in 2005 and Hurricane Sandy struck in 2012.

 The two bars charts show the cost of and number of repairs needed after disasters divided up by the type of repair project. These charts show that spending is disproportionate to number of projects within several categories. Building, road, and bridge repairs are much more costly than recreational/other repairs. It is also interesting to note that the most money was spent on protective measures and that they were also the most abundant type fo project created.
